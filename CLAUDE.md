# WGU Problem-Solving with AI Course

This repo contains the design and materials for a WGU General Education course focused on problem-solving with AI.

## Project Context

Dave Hedengren is collaborating with Emily Lyman at WGU to design a GE course where students learn to identify problems and build AI-powered solutions. The transcripts in this repo capture the initial brainstorming conversations.

## Key Design Principles

- Students should go from scaffolded problem-solving (week 1) to fully independent (week 12)
- The output should be a persistent, deployed solution (website/app) that solves a real problem
- Students use hosted platforms (Lovable, Replit, etc.) — whichever is best/cheapest at the time
- A chatbot/prompt system should help students articulate good problems (students are bad at this)
- Avoid "paint-by-numbers" — the goal is empowerment, not checkbox completion
- Course also covers AI fundamentals (what LLMs are, tokens, sentence prediction)
- Practical AI skills: project timelines, meeting agendas, action item summarization, personalized learning
